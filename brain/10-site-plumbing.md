# Site Plumbing Reference

_Last updated: 2026-04-19_

Combined plumbing documentation for residential and light commercial work that intersects Saunders’ cabinetry scope: kitchen runs, wet bars, laundry, and bath vanities.

## Source PDF

- **`docs/combined-plumbing.pdf`** — full “COMBINED - Plumbing” reference (also listed in Agent Brain → Source Documents).

Use this file for **agent orientation** and cross-checking field conditions. Do not invent code cycles, pipe sizes, or fixture counts that are not in the PDF or confirmed by the plumber of record.

## Intake form impact (`intake.html`)

| Intake area | How plumbing matters |
|-------------|----------------------|
| **Project basics → Site readiness / access notes** | Rough-in dates, wall/floor penetrations, shutoff locations, slab vs. crawl access, hose bib / mop sink conflicts, staging for first-fix vs. trim-out. |
| **Dimensions** | Sink base and panel depths vs. drain centerlines; dishwasher / fridge water boxes; ice maker lines behind panels; clearance for P-traps and disposer flanges. |
| **Vision / narrative** | Fixture style intent (prep sink vs. main bowl, pot filler, filtered water) so cabinet elevations can leave chase space. |

Encourage clients (or Matt) to note **which trades are GC vs. owner-supplied** and whether **as-built** dimensions exist before final Decore field sizes are locked.

## Coordination rules for agents

1. **Cabinet field sizes are net of finish** — plumbing centerlines must be reconciled to **actual box interior** and face-frame/inset reveals, not rough framing only.
2. **Hot and cold isolation** — confirm valve type (1/4-turn vs. multi-turn) and whether trim plates conflict with decorative panels or appliance garages.
3. **Gas + electric** — where the combined PDF covers adjacent scope, flag conflicts early (e.g., downdraft + depth of cabinet run).
4. When the answer is not in this repo or the PDF, say **unknown** and recommend a licensed plumber sign-off.

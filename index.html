01 The Problem

Six pain points costing
real money every week.

01
👁

No real-time project visibility

No single view showing which projects are on track, behind schedule, or burning margin. Problems surface too late.

02
📐

Linear-foot pricing leaves money behind

Flat LF rates don't account for hardware costs, finish complexity, or design time. Hardware as overhead quietly erases profit on high-end jobs.

03
📄

Appliance packets derail design timelines

PDFs arrive mid-project. Model numbers and cut-out specs get manually hunted, and the designer waits. Every delay costs install-date credibility.

04
⏱

Builder time isn't tracked to profitability

You can't know if the most-worked project is your most profitable. A smaller job could be silently eating your best crew hours.

05
🌅

No daily operating system for Matt

No structured morning view of what's at risk, who's idle, which quotes need follow-up — and no automated way to stay ahead of it.

06
💬

Communication runs on memory and calls

Email threads, verbal updates, missed follow-ups. The company's "seamless" promise is hard to keep without systems supporting it.

Project Health Score —
automatic, always on.

Every project self-scores every time it's updated. No manual check-ins, no spreadsheets — a live formula that tells Matt exactly where to look before the day starts.

Staleness penalty — silence costs points
Monday Formula Column — Health Score (0–100) 
IF(OR({Sched Var%} > 10, {Budget Var%} > 10),
  "🔴 At Risk",
  IF(OR({Sched Var%} > 5, {Budget Var%} > 5,
         {Open Tasks%} > 20),
    "🟡 Watch", "🟢 Healthy"))

// Numeric score
100 − ({Sched Var%}×2) − ({Budget Var%}×2)
    − ({Open Tasks%}×1) − ({Days Stale}×1)

🟢 Healthy

All variances under 5%, tasks on track, board updated within 3 days. No action needed — Matt's morning view stays green.

Variance < 5% · Tasks < 20% open · Updated < 3 days

🟡 Watch

One metric is drifting. Project owner gets an auto-notification. Matt reviews at next morning check-in — still recoverable.

Any variance 5–10% · or open tasks 20–35%

🔴 At Risk

A variance exceeded 10%. Matt is alerted immediately. If red persists 2 days, a "Fix Project Risk" task is auto-assigned.

Any variance > 10% · Triggers instant alert + task

  • As-is process map (sales → design → build → install → billing)
  • Inventory of boards, tools, and integrations; risk & dependency list
  • Scope freeze sign-off before design/build spend

Design

Blueprint before build

Days 5–6

  • Monday workspace architecture: boards, groups, columns, views
  • Automation blueprint (triggers, owners, failure paths)
  • Health score formula spec + dashboard wireframe
  • CRM / intake field mapping and quote-to-project handoff

Build

Configure & integrate

Days 7–11

  • Monday configuration, templates, and permissions
  • n8n workflows: email, tasks, follow-ups, milestone signals
  • AI email draft path with approval; Otter → action items (if in scope)
  • QuickBooks milestone hooks tested in sandbox or safe account

Test

Prove on real work

Days 12–13

  • UAT on real or mirror projects: health score, dispatch, CRM
  • Edge cases: overdue tasks, stalled quotes, failed automations
  • Fix list triaged; build complete checkpoint

Train & launch

Adoption & cutover

Days 14–20

  • Loom library: short clips per workflow (reference after we leave)
  • Live working sessions with Matt + leads; cutover date agreed
